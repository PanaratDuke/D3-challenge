Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

Obesity has a positive correlation with poverty, a negative correlation with median income, and no clear correlation with age.

Smoking has a postive correlation with poverty, a positive correlation with age, and a negative correlation with median income. It is ironic that states with older populations have more smokers?

The lack of healthcare correlates positvely with poverty, and negatively with median income. There is no clear correlation with median age.

This brief study might be improved by adding latitude and longitude. Casual observations show that the southeast have problems with obesity, poverty, and a lack of healthcare. There might also be positive correlation between smoking levels and states that grow signicant amounts of tobacco. The factors of poverty and median income, though opposite, were redundant.
